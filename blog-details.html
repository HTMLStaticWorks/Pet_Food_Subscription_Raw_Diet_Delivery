Demystifying Ratios: The 80-10-10 Rule

Published by Dr. Julian Thorne | 5 Min Read | Canine Health

Understanding raw ratios is critical when moving away from commercial dry kibbles. The 80-10-10 guideline is formulated to replicate the nutritional ratios found in a canine's ancestral prey diets.

What is the 80-10-10 Rule?

This rule divides the total daily serving weight of your pet's food into three key biological components:

  • 80% Muscle Meat: The primary energy source containing lean protein, hydration, essential amino acids, and fats.
  • 10% Bone: Supplies vital calcium, phosphorus, magnesium, and marrow minerals in natural, digestively safe structures.
  • 10% Organs (Offal): Replicates the trace vitamins. 5% must consist of liver, and 5% must consist of secreting organs like kidneys, spleen, or pancreas.

Why Commercial Kibble Falls Short

Biologically appropriate raw feeding ensures your pet absorbs active, live enzymes directly from fresh cellular tissue.
